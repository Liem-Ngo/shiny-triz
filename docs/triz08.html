TRIZ Creativity Principle: Counter-Weight

Compensating for disadvantages by combining with advantages.

What is the Counter-Weight Principle?

The Counter-Weight Principle compensates for an object's "harmful weight" or disadvantage by combining it with another object or environmental force that provides a lifting or balancing effect. For example, a parachute slows a fall by using air resistance, or attractive packaging offsets low-quality goods. This principle encourages creative problem-solving by leveraging advantages to neutralize weaknesses.
