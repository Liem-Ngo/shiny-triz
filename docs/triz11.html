About the Principle

The TRIZ principle of "Beforehand Cushioning" involves preparing emergency means in advance to compensate for the relatively low reliability of an object. In this simulation, the emergency lighting system acts as a backup to ensure illumination during a power outage, demonstrating the importance of having safety measures ready beforehand.

Other examples include:

  • Magnetic strip on photographic film to compensate for poor exposure
  • Back-up parachute for skydivers
  • Alternate air system for aircraft instruments
  • Fire alarms and firefighting equipment
  • Lifebuoys and lifeboats on ships
