Illustration of the "Contained Within" Principle

The "Contained Within" principle, also known as the Matriôshka Principle, involves one object containing another object within it, which may contain yet another, and so on.

This TRIZ creativity principle is inspired by Russian Matriôshka dolls, where each doll nests a smaller doll inside.

Real-world Examples:

  • Telescopic antennas (tubes within tubes)
  • Collapsible camping cups
  • A hammer with nails stored in its hollow handle
  • Pocket-sized fishing rod that extends from a compact form
  • Nesting cookware sets where pots fit inside each other
  • Extendable selfie sticks

The concept can also apply to objects moving through others, like water through pipelines or trains through tunnels.
